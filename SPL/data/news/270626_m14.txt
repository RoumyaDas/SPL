🏏 Match 14 – LSG vs RCB: A Spin Web Snags LSG as RCB Cruise

In a contest tailor-made for spinners, RCB outfoxed LSG on a turning track to secure a comfortable victory. After winning the toss and electing to bowl, RCB's spin trio of Keshav Maharaj, Wanindu Hasaranga, and Sandeep Lamichhane spun a web around the LSG batting line-up. Despite a brisk start from Shubman Gill and Finn Allen, wickets tumbled at regular intervals. A late cameo from Shubhang Hegde and some lusty blows from Ravindra Jadeja and Kuldeep Yadav helped LSG crawl to a modest 145/8, a total that looked well below par on a surface that was tricky but not unplayable.

Chasing 146, RCB openers Chris Gayle and Atharva Taide got off to a solid start, adding 93 runs for the first wicket. Gayle looked in ominous touch, smashing 45 off just 29 balls, while Taide played the anchor role with a steady 47. Although LSG's bowlers, led by Henry Ssenyondo and Hardik Pandya, struck back with quick wickets in the middle overs, the chase was never in doubt. The arrival of Wanindu Hasaranga at the death proved to be the knockout punch, as he smashed two towering sixes to seal the chase with 8 balls to spare. RCB's disciplined bowling, followed by a composed batting performance, saw them win by 5 wickets, with Hasaranga's all-round brilliance earning him the Player of the Match.
