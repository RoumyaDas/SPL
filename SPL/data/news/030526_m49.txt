📄 **Match 49: RR vs DC – The Spin Web That Strangled Royals**

On a pitch assisting spin by nearly 3x, RR won the toss and chose to bat first — a decision that backfired spectacularly. DC’s rookie left-arm spinner Ashwin Shelke (2/12) produced a dream opening over: Jaiswal (0), then Dube (0) off consecutive deliveries. Rickelton (0) fell next over. RR were 4/3. Aniket Verma (27 off 10) and Glenn Phillips (56 off 26) launched a counterattack, but wickets never stopped falling. From 52/3, RR collapsed to 110 all out in 15.1 overs. Left-arm spinner Harsh Dubey (2/5 from 2 overs) and off-spinner Markram (3/21) choked the life out of the innings.

Chasing 111, DC openers Maxwell (48 off 23) and Markram (0) had contrasting fates. Two wickets fell early, but Maxwell kept attacking, smoking eight fours and a six. Tarang Gohel (38 off 18) played the perfect second fiddle. Despite a flurry of late wickets, DC cruised to 111/8 in 13.4 overs, winning with 38 balls to spare. A spin-dominant demolition.

**Winner:** DC  
**Player of the Match:** GD Phillips (RR) – for his lone-hand 56 in a wrecked innings
