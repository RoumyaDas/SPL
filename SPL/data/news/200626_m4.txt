# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 4: PBKS vs MI – A Bowler's Masterclass

The fourth match of the season witnessed an absolute bowling clinic from MI's Shivam Shukla, who dismantled PBKS with a sensational six-wicket haul.

MI won the toss and opted to bat first, a decision that initially seemed to backfire. PBKS's bowlers, led by the economical AR Ramjani, kept things tight. Ramjani was simply outstanding, finishing with figures of 3/25 from his four overs, consistently beating the bat and building pressure from the very first ball.

MI's innings was held together by a defiant knock from Urvil Patel. He anchored the innings with a masterful 96* off just 58 balls, finding the gaps with precision and holding the fort as wickets tumbled around him. He received valuable cameos from MR Marsh (30 off 19) and a late blitz from Shivam Shukla, who smashed 16 off just 6 balls to propel MI to a competitive total of 168/7. The innings was a classic tale of one man standing tall while the rest faltered against a disciplined PBKS bowling attack.

Chasing 169, PBKS needed a solid start, and they got just that. V Suryavanshi and MS Pepper put on 47 runs in the Powerplay, with Pepper looking particularly fluent. Suryavanshi fell for a brisk 20, but Pepper continued to fight, reaching a well-deserved half-century. At 89/1, PBKS were cruising and firmly in the driver's seat.

Then came the collapse.

Shivam Shukla, who was brought into the attack, turned the game on its head. He started by removing the dangerous MS Pepper, LBW for 50. But he wasn't done. He ripped through the middle and lower order, claiming the wickets of R Smaran, Vansh Bedi, JJ Smit, S Gopal, AR Ramjani, and CV Varun. His figures of 6/24 from just 3.3 overs were nothing short of magical. He was ably supported by JR Hazlewood, who chipped in with 3 wickets, including the crucial scalp of SP Narine.

PBKS's chase, which once looked so promising, fell apart spectacularly. They lost their last 6 wickets for just 14 runs, collapsing from 148/4 to 160 all out. It was a stunning turnaround, a reminder of how quickly fortunes can change in T20 cricket. MI defended their total with sheer brilliance, and Shivam Shukla's spell will be remembered as one of the great individual performances of the season.
