# 🏏 SPL S07, Match 56 – GT vs LSG: The Centurion Who Almost Pulled It Off

The pitch was balanced between spin and pace, and LSG won the toss and chose to bowl first — a decision that looked sharp for about four overs. Then GT's batters took over.

Samson went into overdrive, plundering 81 off just 37 balls with 9 fours and 4 sixes, treating pace and spin like a net session. Inglis fell early, but Kulkarni and Naman Dhir kept the scoreboard humming. Just when LSG thought they had a grip, Brevis arrived and blasted an unbeaten 50 off 28. H Ssenyondo’s 4-wicket haul kept things from exploding completely, but GT still piled up **219/6** — a daunting total.

LSG’s chase began with Gill in god-mode. He was untouchable: 102 off 51 balls, 8 fours, 4 sixes, and a strike rate of exactly 200. Mousley fell cheaply, Watson bagged a duck, but Gill kept finding partners — Porel (28), Jay Gohil (42) — and kept LSG in the hunt. At 172/4 with 4 overs left, victory was a heartbeat away.

Then the wheels came off.

Harshit Rana (3/36) and Sufiyan Muqeem (3/51) strangled the middle order. Gill departed for a stunning 💯, Archer and Kuldeep Yadav fell in quick succession, and LSG crashed from needing 2 off 5 to falling **1 run short** — finishing on 218/8.

GT won by 1 run. In a T20. Absolute madness.

**Winner:** GT by 1 run  
**Player of the Match:** Shubman Gill (LSG) – for a century in a losing cause
