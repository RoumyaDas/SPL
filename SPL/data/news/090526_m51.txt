🏏 SPL S07, Match 51: RR vs GT – A Run-Fest & A Record Chase

The pitch at RR was a graveyard for spinners and a paradise for pace, offering 1.8x more assistance to quick bowlers. GT won the toss and elected to bowl first, a decision that looked sharp early but was tested to its limits by the end of the night.

RR’s innings started with intent but stumbled early. Virat Kohli (20 off 17) looked scratchy against the new ball before being cleaned up by a sharp delivery from Harshit Rana. The big blows, however, came from Shivam Dube (55 off 33) and the explosive Aniket Verma (63* off 37). Dube targeted the shorter boundaries with 8 fours and 2 sixes, while Verma played a mature hand, punishing anything loose. Despite a flurry of wickets in the death, including Jaiswal (32 off 15) and Phillips, RR posted a formidable 204/5.

Chasing 205, GT’s chase was nothing short of a heist. Sanju Samson (6 off 2) departed second ball, caught behind off Karan Lal, sending shockwaves. But then came Naman Dhir. He walked in at 13/2 and played what might be the innings of the season. Facing a potent pace attack, Dhir smashed 94 off just 44 balls (9 fours, 4 sixes), treating the death overs like a net session. He fell LBW to Natarajan for 94, but the damage was done. Vansh Nigam (35* off 17) and Dewald Brevis (32* off 15) finished the job with 4 balls to spare, sealing a thrilling 4-wicket win.

GT chased down 205 with an over to spare, riding on one of the greatest knocks in SPL history.

Result: GT wins by 4 wickets (19.2 overs)
Player of the Match: Naman Dhir (GT)
