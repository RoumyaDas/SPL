📝 **SPL S07, Match 65: DC vs RR – A Spin-Fueled Thriller**

The pitch was a rank turner, assisting spin by over three times as much as pace. DC won the toss, elected to bat, and proceeded to unleash absolute mayhem.

GJ Maxwell came out with fireworks, hammering 11 fours and a six in his 64 off just 29 balls. But the real story was **N Sindhu**. Walking in early after a wicket, he held the innings together with breathtaking elegance and power. He remained *Not Out* on 105 off 49 balls, hitting 11 fours and 5 sixes in a century that redefined dominance. A late cameo from Rituraj Sharma (49 off 22) and a six from Russell pushed DC to a mammoth **240/5**.

In reply, RR’s chase never found flight. Openers Kohli (16) and Salt (14) fell cheaply. While YBK Jaiswal fought a lone battle with a blazing 61 off 28, wickets kept tumbling at the other end. DC’s spinners — **AK Markram (3/22)** and **Harsh Dubey (3/36)** — ripped through the middle and lower order, sharing 6 wickets between them. RR folded for **188** in 19.1 overs, falling 52 runs short.

A complete masterclass from DC. Sindhu’s century will be remembered for seasons.
