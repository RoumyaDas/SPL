🏏 **Match 67: SRH vs RCB – Summary**

RCB won the toss and elected to bat first on a pace-friendly pitch at SRH. Openers PD Salt (23) and Salil Arora (38) laid a solid foundation, but it was the explosive PWH de Silva who stole the show with a breathtaking 70 off just 34 balls. Despite wickets falling at regular intervals, a late fireworks from Karan Shinde (37* off 12) propelled RCB to a commanding 212/5. C Sakariya was the pick of SRH bowlers with two wickets.

In response, SRH openers MR Marsh (28) and Tushar Raheja (52) kept the chase alive with a steady partnership. However, the introduction of seamer Ravi Teja turned the tide dramatically. He ripped through the SRH batting lineup with a sensational five-wicket haul, triggering a massive collapse. Ashutosh Sharma chipped in with three wickets as SRH crumbled from a promising position to be bowled out for just 164. RCB secured a dominant 49-run victory.
