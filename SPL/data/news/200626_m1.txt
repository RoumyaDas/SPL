# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 1: RCB vs SRH – A Tale of Two Innings

The season opener served up a brutal reminder that T20 cricket can be a cruel mistress.

RCB won the toss and elected to bowl first, a decision that looked inspired early on. Yash Singh, making his SPL debut, struck in his second over to remove the dangerous P Simran Singh, while PWH de Silva had already accounted for SU Parakh. At 24/2, RCB had their foot on SRH's throat.

Then came the KL Rahul show.

Walking in at number three, Rahul carved out a masterclass that will be remembered for a long time. He was imperious, finding gaps at will and punishing anything loose. He found an able partner in Kartik Sharma, who played the aggressor's role to perfection, smashing a 33-ball 54. Their 85-run stand for the third wicket set the platform for a brutal finish.

And finish they did. VR Iyer, coming in after Kartik's dismissal, played a sensational cameo of 48* off just 21 balls. Rahul remained unbeaten on a magnificent 104* off 54 deliveries, studded with 10 fours and 5 sixes. The duo plundered 79 runs in the last 6 overs, propelling SRH to a colossal 225/3 in their 20 overs.

The chase was always going to be a mountain to climb for RCB. Chris Gayle, as he often does, came out swinging. He smashed 42 off just 18 balls, and PWH de Silva added a brisk 32, as RCB raced to 64/1 in the Powerplay.

But the dream quickly turned into a nightmare.

The introduction of Rasikh Salam and M Markande into the attack triggered a monumental collapse. Salam, bowling with pinpoint accuracy and venom, ripped through the middle order with a sensational spell of 4/2 in his 2 overs. Markande was equally devastating, claiming 3/33.

From 64/1, RCB lost 9 wickets for just 67 runs. The collapse was swift and brutal. Wickets tumbled in clusters: first Gayle, then de Silva, then the dangerous Priyansh Arya. The procession never stopped. Sharma, Patidar, Pathak, Sharma (again), Holder, Maharaj, and Lamichhane all fell cheaply. RCB were bowled out for a paltry 131 in just 15 overs.

It was a statement victory for SRH, a complete performance that showcased both their batting depth and their incredible bowling attack. KL Rahul's century anchored the innings, but the bowling, led by the exceptional Rasikh Salam, utterly dismantled the RCB chase.
