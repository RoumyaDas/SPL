# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 5: RR vs CSK – Spin Web Strangles Run-Fest

The fifth match of the season was a classic battle between bat and ball, where CSK's spinners spun a web that proved too tight for RR to escape.

CSK won the toss and elected to bowl first, and the decision paid immediate dividends. The pitch, offering significant turn, was tailor-made for their spin attack. Aniket Verma started explosively for RR, smashing 45 off just 23 balls, while GD Phillips chipped in with a blistering 28 off 12. The duo took RR to a promising 78/2 at the end of the Powerplay.

Then came the collapse.

Tanay Thyagarajan, bowling his first spell, turned the game on its head. He removed the dangerous Phillips, castled YBK Jaiswal, and trapped Atharva Ankolekar LBW, finishing with figures of 3/17 from his four overs. Fazalhaq Farooqi provided the perfect support, picking up two crucial wickets of Aniket Verma and KA Pollard. RR, who looked set for a big total, were reduced to 109/7, and could only manage a below-par 143/9 in their 20 overs. The innings was a testament to CSK's disciplined bowling, where run-scoring became an arduous task against the turning ball.

Chasing a modest 144, CSK's chase got off to a disastrous start. Vraj Patel, making his debut, struck with his very first ball, removing the dangerous Tilak Varma for a golden duck. Mohammad Ghazanfar then struck twice in his first over, dismissing F du Plessis and KK Nair, reducing CSK to a precarious 10/3.

But then, the rescue act began. AM Rahane and H Klaasen steadied the ship with a gritty partnership. Rahane played a composed knock of 29, while Klaasen anchored the innings with a well-crafted 42. They took CSK to 60/4 in the 7th over. After Rahane's departure, Tanush Kotian walked in and played the innings of his life. He smashed a breathtaking 48 off just 29 balls, taking the game away from RR. He found able support from Klaasen and then from Tanay Thyagarajan, who chipped in with a vital cameo.

CSK lost a few wickets in the death overs, but RE van der Merwe sealed the chase in style with a six off the first ball of the 18th over. The victory was clinical, a complete performance where the bowlers set up the win and the batsmen finished the job. Tanay Thyagarajan's all-round brilliance earned him the Player of the Match award, but it was a team effort that showcased CSK's dominance in spin-friendly conditions.
