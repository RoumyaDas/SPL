# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 35 – SRH vs GT: Duckett & Parakh Script a Thrilling Chase

The sun had set on a classic batting beauty in Hyderabad, but the heat was just rising. SRH won the toss, and their captain made the bold call to bowl first — a decision that would be tested as GT’s innings unfolded.

GT’s innings began with a tremor. **D Padikkal** fell for a golden duck, caught by Rasikh Salam off **NT Ellis**. **JP Inglis**, the impact batter, arrived and perished immediately — trapped LBW by **Ravi Teja** for just 4. The scoreboard read 6/2, and GT’s fans held their breath.

What followed was a rescue act of pure grit. **SV Samson** anchored a jittery ship with a fluent 30 before becoming Rasikh Salam’s first victim — clean bowled. **D Brevis** looked in devastating touch, hammering boundaries at will, but fell for a well-made 31 after a sharp catch by NT Ellis. At 74/4, the innings was in a shambles.

Enter the resurgence. **V Nigam**, calm as a monk, stitched together a vital 30-ball 29, but the real show began when **Nithish Kumar Reddy** walked in. In a masterclass of calculated aggression, he smoked 57 off just 30 balls, raining sixes and fours to pull GT to a fighting total of 172/8. **NT Ellis** and **Ravi Teja** were the standout bowlers for SRH, picking up two wickets each, but it was Ellis’s incisive bowling in the death that limited GT’s acceleration.

With 173 needed, SRH’s chase started like a steamroller. **P Simran Singh** went bazooka mode, smashing 20 off just 9 balls. But a sharp run-out by D Brevis cut his cameo short. The wicket brought **BM Duckett** and **SU Parakh** together — and together, they produced a partnership of pure poetry.

**Duckett**, who had been on a lean patch, silenced all critics with a stunning 76 off 51 balls. He blended elegance with brute force, hitting five sixes and finding gaps at will. **SU Parakh** anchored the other end brilliantly with a poised 52, rotating strike and punishing the loose deliveries. The duo took SRH to the cusp of victory.

GT fought back. **J Overton** was the warrior with the ball, claiming a three-wicket haul, including the prized scalp of Duckett and a stunning double-wicket over in the 19th. But it was too late. **KL Rahul** walked in, cool as ice, smashed a quick 16, and alongside **VR Iyer** — who ended the game with a monstrous six — took SRH home with 5 balls to spare.

A nerve-shredding thriller. A chase to remember. And **BM Duckett** walks away with the Player of the Match for his majestic knock.

**SRH wins by 6 wickets!**
