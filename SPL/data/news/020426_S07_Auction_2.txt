SPL S07: Full Team Strength Report (Batting + Bowling)

### **Headline: Balanced Squads Take the Lead as Data Reveals True Contenders**

As teams lock in their squads post the SPL S07 Auction, a deeper look into **combined batting and bowling data** reveals a clear separation between *well-rounded contenders* and *flawed units*.

---

## **Top Tier: Complete Teams (Batting + Bowling Alignment)**

### **GT — The Most Complete Side**

GT emerges as the **most balanced and efficient team overall**.

* **Batting:** Best efficiency (high SR + Avg, low dot %)
* **Bowling:** Strong control metrics — consistent economy + wicket-taking spread
* **Key Trait:** No visible weakness across departments

**Why they’re dangerous:**
They don’t rely on one phase — they win games across all 40 overs.

---

### **SRH — The Silent Contender**

SRH doesn’t dominate any single metric but ranks **top 3 in both batting and bowling stability**.

* **Batting:** Strong SR + reliable averages
* **Bowling:** Disciplined, low volatility attack
* **Key Trait:** Consistency across matches

**Why they’re dangerous:**
They’re the least likely team to collapse in either innings.

---

## **Batting-Heavy but Bowling Dependent**

### **DC — Firepower with Bowling Question Marks**

* **Batting:** Highest volume + depth
* **Bowling:** Slightly inconsistent in economy and wicket conversion

**Risk:**
Can outscore opponents — but may struggle to defend totals.

---

### **RR — High Ceiling, High Variance**

* **Batting:** Aggressive, high SR
* **Bowling:** Inconsistent control metrics

**Risk:**
Matches may swing wildly — not built for control.

---

## **Bowling-Driven / Control Teams**

### **CSK — Structure Over Flair**

* **Batting:** Moderate, slightly conservative
* **Bowling:** Strong control (economy, dot pressure)

**Strength:**
Can choke oppositions in low-to-mid scoring games

**Concern:**
May not chase big totals effectively

---

### **KKR — Efficient but Thin**

* **Batting:** High average, low depth
* **Bowling:** Decent efficiency, but not dominant

**Risk:**
Over-reliance on core players in both departments

---

## **Unbalanced Teams**

### **MI — Talent Without Output**

* **Batting:** Poor efficiency (low SR, high dot %)
* **Bowling:** Inconsistent wicket-taking + pressure

**Issue:**
Underperformance in both departments → lacks match control

---

### **PBKS — Average Across Both Ends**

* **Batting:** Mid-tier across all metrics
* **Bowling:** Similar mid-level impact

**Issue:**
No standout strength to win games decisively

---

### **LSG — Struggles in Both Phases**

* **Batting:** Low SR + high dot %
* **Bowling:** Weak pressure metrics, inconsistent wickets

**Issue:**
Falls behind in both scoring and control

---

## **Wildcard**

### **RCB — High Quality, Low Sample**

* **Batting:** Elite numbers (avg + SR)
* **Bowling:** Decent, but limited data

**Reality:**
Could be top-tier — but data uncertainty is huge

# **Key Insight (Most Important)**

This season won’t be decided by:

* The most explosive batting lineup
* Or the best bowling attack

It will be decided by **teams that minimize dot balls *and* maintain bowling pressure simultaneously**.

Right now, **GT and SRH are the only teams ticking both boxes consistently**.
