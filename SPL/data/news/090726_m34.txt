# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 34 – DC vs CSK: A Run-Fest for the Ages

The stage was set for a showdown of titans, and the pitch was a spinners' graveyard — spin was predicted to assist 4x more than pace, but the batters had other plans. CSK won the toss and elected to bowl first, a decision that backfired spectacularly as DC unleashed an absolute carnage with the bat.

DC's innings began in disaster when **GJ Maxwell** was bowled for a golden duck off the very first ball by **Geet Puri**. The crowd gasped. But what followed was a masterclass in destruction. **SA Yadav**, entering at number 3, played one of the finest knocks of the tournament. He was elegance and brutality personified, smashing a breathtaking century off just 52 balls, laced with 11 fours and 5 sixes. He found an able partner in **JC Buttler**, who played the perfect foil with an explosive 58 off 30 balls. The real carnage, however, came from **N Sindhu**, who walked in late and smoked a jaw-dropping 48 off just 15 balls — a strike rate of 320 that left the CSK bowlers shell-shocked. DC finished with a mammoth 250/3, a total that seemed impossible to chase.

CSK's chase started like a dream, with **Tilak Varma** and **JM Vince** stitching a record-breaking 181-run opening partnership. **Vince**, playing only his 4th SPL game, looked like a veteran, scoring a fluent 74 off 41 balls. **Tilak Varma** was a man on a mission; he brought up his century in style and finished with a monstrous 113 off 57 balls, decorated with 10 fours and 4 sixes. The duo kept CSK in the hunt, even as the required rate climbed past 14.

But the tide turned when **Harsh Dubey** struck, dismissing Vince LBW. What followed was a collapse — Varma fell to **AK Markram**, and Dubey ran through the middle order, picking up 3 crucial wickets including the golden ducks of KK Nair and RD Gaikwad. **T Stubbs** provided some late fireworks with a cameo of 20 off 6 balls, but it was far too little, far too late. CSK managed 236/6, falling short by 14 runs in a game that was pure, unadulterated entertainment.

A record-breaking total, a breathtaking chase, and a deserving win for DC.

**DC wins by 14 runs!**
