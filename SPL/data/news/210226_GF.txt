**🏆 SPL Season 6 – GRAND FINALE: Royal Challengers Bangalore vs Mumbai Indians – Match Summary**

In a finale for the ages at Lucknow, Royal Challengers Bangalore clinched their maiden SPL title in the most dramatic fashion, defeating Mumbai Indians by just 2 runs in a high-voltage thriller. On a surface that offered equal assistance to pace and spin, the match swung like a pendulum, keeping 50,000 fans on the edge of their seats until the final ball.

**RCB's Innings: Priyansh Arya's Masterclass & The Late Surge**

Asked to bat first, RCB lost Phil Salt early, bowled by Shivam Shukla for 5. But Priyansh Arya (67 off 38) and Wanindu Hasaranga (26 off 15) launched a sensational counterattack, adding 57 runs in just 5 overs. Arya was in imperious form, peppering the boundaries with 10 fours and a six, looking untroubled against both pace and spin. He brought up his fifty off just 30 balls – his third fifty of the playoffs. Hasaranga fell to Trent Boult, but Arya continued, before falling LBW to Hardik Pandya for a brilliantly crafted 67. Salil Arora (30 off 22) provided able support but was bowled by debutant MW Short. At 142/5 after 15 overs, RCB were in danger of falling below par. But Jitesh Sharma (41* off 20) and Karan Shinde (27* off 16) unleashed a brutal assault in the death overs, plundering 64 runs in the last 5 overs. Jitesh's six-hitting spree (3 sixes) and Shinde's late fireworks propelled RCB to a formidable 206/5. Trent Boult was the pick of MI bowlers with 2/39.

**MI's Chase: Taide's Heroics & Hasaranga's Match-Winning Spell**

Chasing 207 for the title, MI openers Abhishek Sharma and Atharva Taide started like a house on fire. They raced to 60/0 in the powerplay, with Abhishek looking particularly dangerous, scoring 53 off 34 balls. Just when MI looked in complete control, Wanindu Hasaranga produced a stunning over that turned the game on its head. In the 10th over, he first trapped Abhishek LBW for 53, then removed Nicholas Pooran (6) and MW Short (2) in consecutive deliveries, reducing MI from 86/0 to 97/3 in the span of 4 balls. The stadium erupted. But Atharva Taide (91* off 56) stood tall, playing the innings of his life. He found an unlikely ally in Urvil Patel (46* off 26), and the duo added an unbeaten 107 runs off just 60 balls for the 4th wicket. They kept MI in the hunt, smashing boundaries at will.

With 19 needed off the final over bowled by Ravi Teja, the equation see-sawed dramatically. Taide took a single first ball. Urvil then smashed two consecutive sixes, bringing the equation down to 6 runs off 3 balls. A single followed, then Taide took another single, leaving Urvil on strike with 4 runs needed off the last ball. The stadium was deafening. Ravi Teja, under immense pressure, bowled a wide – bringing the equation down to 3 runs off 1 ball. With the title on the line, Urvil swung across the line, attempting a wild heave, but completely missed the ball. The ball thudded into the keeper's gloves. A dot ball. Silence. Then eruption. RCB had defended 3 runs off the last ball, winning their maiden title by just 2 runs in the most dramatic finish imaginable.

Hasaranga's sensational spell of 3/40, including that match-turning over, earned him the Player of the Match in the final. Atharva Taide's heroic 91* will be remembered as one of the greatest knocks in a losing cause in a final.
