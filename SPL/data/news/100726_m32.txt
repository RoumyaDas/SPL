# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 32 – RR vs RCB: A Run-Heist at the Death

The pace-friendly track in Jaipur promised entertainment, and boy, did it deliver. RR won the toss and opted to bowl first, a decision that seemed brilliant when **CH Gayle** was sent packing for a golden duck in the very first over by **L Ngidi**. RCB were in early trouble, but **A Mhatre** steadied the ship with a gritty 45, while **Priyansh Arya** smashed a quickfire 26 before falling to the same bowler.

Then came the onslaught. **Abhishek Pathak** walked in at number 5 and played an absolute blinder, hammering 72 off just 30 balls at a strike rate of 240. With timely cameos from **JM Sharma** and a few lusty blows from the tail, RCB recovered to post 211/8 — a total that seemed defendable but would test the best of chases.

RR's response was nothing short of breathtaking. **Aniket Verma** came out with intent, launching **M Pathirana** for 24 runs in the very first over. He was in sublime touch, racing to 92 off just 43 balls with 8 fours and 5 sixes, before being caught on the cusp of a well-deserved century. **S Dube** played the perfect second fiddle with 29 off 16, and **GD Phillips** chipped in with a rapid 21.

RCB's bowlers fought valiantly — **SS Ghosh** bagged a four-wicket haul, and **HJ Manenti** grabbed three, including the wickets of S Dube, Sakibul Gani, and Junaid Siddique in quick succession. But RR never lost their way. With 15 needed off 15 and wickets tumbling, **KA Pollard** held his nerve, anchoring the chase with an unbeaten 18. He finished it off in style, hitting the winning boundary in the 19th over.

What a game! Momentum swung like a pendulum, but RR emerged victorious in the end. **Aniket Verma** walked away as the Player of the Match for his explosive knock.

**RR wins by 9 wickets (with 11 balls to spare)!**
