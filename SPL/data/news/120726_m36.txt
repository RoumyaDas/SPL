# 🏏 SPL S08 – Match 36: PBKS vs RR – A Tale of Two Halves

PBKS won the toss and elected to bat first on a deck that was expected to assist spin heavily. They got off to a cautious start, but Mohammad Ghazanfar struck twice in the 5th over to remove both openers and put RR on top. However, Vansh Bedi walked in at number four and changed the complexion of the game. He launched a brutal assault, smashing 77 off just 35 balls, while Shashank Singh played a calm anchor role with 33. Their 108-run partnership took PBKS from a precarious 36/4 to a commanding position.

But just when a 180+ total seemed inevitable, PBKS suffered a dramatic collapse. Atharva Ankolekar and Karan Lal ran through the lower order with sharp spin bowling, picking up 3 and 2 wickets respectively, as PBKS lost their last 6 wickets for just 20 runs, folding for 164 in 18.5 overs. Ghazanfar was the star with the ball early on, finishing with 3 wickets, while Ankolekar’s 3-fer in the middle overs turned the tide.

Chasing 165, RR's chase began steadily but they never truly got going. Aniket Verma looked dangerous with a fluent 45, but the required rate kept climbing. Spinners dominated the second innings as well, with PBKS using seven bowling options, all of whom picked up a wicket each. Narine was the pick with 2 wickets, while the others chipped in at crucial intervals. RR kept losing wickets at regular intervals, and despite a late cameo from YBK Jaiswal (34 off 12) that brought the equation down to 23 off the last over, Narine held his nerve with two wickets in the final over to seal a 16-run victory for PBKS.

A seesaw battle that swung wildly in both innings, but PBKS’s early recovery and disciplined bowling in the death overs proved to be the difference.
