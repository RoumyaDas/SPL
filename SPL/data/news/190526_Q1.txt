🏆 SPL S07 – QUALIFIER 1 | RCB vs SRH | MI Ground

TOSS: RCB elected to BAT first on a pitch offering 3x pace assistance.

FIRST INNINGS – THE GAYLE STORM

RCB openers walked in under pressure. Priyansh Arya (11 off 7) showed intent but fell LBW to Rasikh Salam in the 2nd over. Enter CH Gayle. What followed was a masterclass in chase-building. Gayle found support from PWH de Silva (27 off 14, SR 192.86), but wickets tumbled around him – Salil Arora (4), JM Sharma (7), Abhishek Pathak (19). At 116/4 in the 12th over, RCB looked vulnerable.

Then came the death overs carnage.

Gayle, who had cruised to 70 off 45, shifted gears. He launched Rasikh Salam for 30 runs in the final over – 6,4,6,4,4,6 – leaving the bowler with figures of 3/67. Gayle finished with 121* off 61 (12 fours, 6 sixes), his highest score of the season. RCB posted 204/7 – their 3rd highest total of the tournament.

SECOND INNINGS – THE HEIST

SRH needed 205. Sahil Parakh fell early (3 off 4), but P Simran Singh (31 off 12, 3 sixes) came out swinging. He fell LBW to Manenti in the 5th over, but KL Rahul (45 off 28) steadied the ship alongside Kartik Sharma (27 off 16). At 100/3 in the 10th over, SRH needed 105 off 60 – gettable but tense.

Then Rahul fell. Then Kartik fell. At 120/4, the equation read 85 off 54.

VR Iyer walked in. He had scored 297 runs in his last 10 innings at a staggering SR of 196.69. He found an unlikely ally in JC Buttler (25 off 13, 3 sixes) and then in tail-ender Tripuresh Singh (15* off 9). Iyer smashed 46* off 23 (4 sixes) and, with 3 runs needed, launched SS Ghosh into the stands to seal the chase.

SRH chased 205 in 18.1 overs.

A Gayle century. A Iyer masterclass. SRH march to the final.
