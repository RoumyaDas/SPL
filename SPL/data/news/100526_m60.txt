# 🏏 SPL S07, Match 60 – RCB vs SRH: The 233-Run Mountain That Crushed a Team

RCB won the toss and chose to bowl first on a balanced pitch. That decision lasted exactly three balls before Sahil Parakh and P Simran Singh started their assault.

Parakh smashed **67 off 39 balls** (10 fours, 2 sixes) while Simran Singh played the anchor role to perfection — **98* off 53** with 7 fours and 5 sixes, falling just two runs short of a well-deserved century. KL Rahul (9) and Kartik Sharma (31 off 13) came and went, but VR Iyer's insane cameo of **26 off 7 balls** (5 fours, 1 six) in the death pushed SRH to a monstrous **233/3**.

RCB's bowlers were destroyed: de Silva 0/56, Scaria 0/53, Abrar Ahmed 1/54. A bowling card that belonged in a horror movie.

The chase was over before it began.

Akshat Raghuwanshi (20) and PWH de Silva (20) showed brief fight, but Rasikh Salam produced a spell for the ages: **4/10 in 3 overs**, absolutely demolishing the middle and lower order. Pandya (2/3), Tripuresh Singh (2/29), Zampa (1/12), and Ellis (1/10) turned the screws.

RCB collapsed to **66 all out in 12 overs**. The required rate was impossible, the batting was nonexistent, and the scoreboard read like a typo.

SRH won by 167 runs. A massacre.

**Winner:** SRH by 167 runs  
**Player of the Match:** P Simran Singh (SRH) – for his magnificent 98*
