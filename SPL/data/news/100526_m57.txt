# 🏏 SPL S07, Match 57 – RR vs MI: The Dube Show & A Chase That Died Screaming

MI won the toss and bowled first on a pitch that loved pace 2.6x more than spin. That decision aged like milk.

Salt and Rickelton started steadily, but the real carnage began when S Dube walked in. He smashed 60 off 35 balls with 4 sixes, holding one end together while wickets fell around him. Phillips (27* off 10) and Karthik (14* off 6) then unleashed hell in the death, dragging RR to **214/5** — a total that felt 20 runs above par.

MI's chase began like a forest fire. Tushar Raheja hammered 42 off just 14 balls (3 sixes) and Urvil Patel added 21 off 11. At 64/1 in the 4th over, MI were ahead of the rate and dreaming. Then Sakariya struck. Then Nagwaswalla. Then Natarajan.

The middle order collapsed like a house of cards. Marsh (11), Smaran (32), Iyer (32) — all got starts, none finished. From 64/1 to 104/4 to 172/6... the required rate ballooned from 9 to 14 to 22. Tanwar threw his bat for 17 off 7, but Natarajan cleaned up the tail with 3 wickets. MI bowled out for **199** in 19.5 overs.

**RR won by 15 runs** — a chase that promised everything and delivered nothing.

**Winner:** RR by 15 runs  
**Player of the Match:** S Dube (RR) – for a match-defining 60
