📋 **Match 31 – SRH vs RCB (Venue: SRH)**

SRH elected to bat first on a pitch offering 3x more assistance to pace, and their openers responded with intent. Prabhsimran Singh played the anchor role to perfection, holding the innings together with a sublime 91 off just 47 balls. He found able support from the middle order, but RCB’s bowlers kept striking at regular intervals. SH Johnson and HJ Manenti were relentless — both grabbing four wickets each as SRH collapsed from a promising position to be bowled out for 191 in 18.4 overs. A competitive total, but perhaps 15-20 short.

In response, RCB lost early wickets and looked rattled at 36/3. Then came the turning point — PWH de Silva exploded with a fiery 58 off 22 balls, smashing five sixes. Even after his departure, Chirag Jani played the innings of his life — an unbeaten 57 off 24 balls under pressure. With 2 runs needed off the final two overs, RCB held their nerve and chased down the target with 10 balls to spare, finishing at 192/6. A chase masterclass built on two stunning counter-attacks. 🏏⚡

**Player of the Match:** P Simran Singh (SRH) — for his valiant 91 in a losing cause.
