# 🏏 SPL Season 08, Match 6 – RCB vs RR

In a brutal display of power-hitting, **RR** chased down a mammoth 218 with 11 balls to spare, dismantling RCB on a pace-friendly track. What started as a chaotic RCB innings ended in a procession, only for their bowlers to face an even greater assault.

RCB's innings was a tale of two halves — CH Gayle (59 off 25) bludgeoned the new ball with 11 fours and 2 sixes, but wickets tumbled like dominos around him. C Sakariya (4/38) and T Natarajan (3/22) sliced through the middle and lower order, triggering a collapse that saw RCB stumble from 150/4 to 165/8 before a late cameo from JM Sharma (45 off 16) pushed them to 217 all out.

The chase, however, was a different beast. PD Salt (67 off 34) and Aniket Verma (34 off 17) laid the foundation with a brutal opening stand. Even after Verma fell, Salt kept the accelerator down before perishing for a well-made half-century. That brought S Dube to the crease — and he simply refused to leave. His unbeaten 65 off 35 was a lesson in controlled aggression, finding boundaries at will. With YBK Jaiswal (32* off 11) providing the finishing fireworks, RR romped home with 11 deliveries to spare.

RR turned the tables in stunning fashion, out-hitting RCB in their own den.

**Player of the Match:** C Sakariya – 4/38, the wrecker-in-chief who triggered the RCB collapse.
