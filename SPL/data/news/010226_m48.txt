Toss: MI opted to bat first on a spin-friendly pitch. They posted a massive 202/4, built on a fiery 66-run opening stand between Abhishek Sharma (46 off 22) and Urvil Patel (40 off 20). N Pooran (39* off 30) and Ishan Kishan (21* off 12) provided a strong finish. CSK's bowlers shared the wickets.

In reply, CSK's chase was anchored by a solid 99-run partnership between HS Desai (54 off 39) and Tilak Varma (46 off 23). However, MI's Noor Ahmad triggered a dramatic collapse with a sensational spell, taking 4 wickets including a triple-wicket maiden over (10th over) that left CSK reeling at 119/6. With 84 needed off 42 balls, the match seemed lost.

Enter MS Dhoni (41* off 21) and Suryansh Shedge (44* off 23). The duo staged a breathtaking counter-attack, smashing 84 runs in the last 6.2 overs. They needed 15 off the final over from HH Pandya, and with ice-cool nerves, they scored them, finishing with a six on the last ball to seal a stunning victory by 4 wickets. Despite the loss, Noor Ahmad's 4/31 earned him Player of the Match.

CSK won a classic El Clasico thriller on the final ball.
