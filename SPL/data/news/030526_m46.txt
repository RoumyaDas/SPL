📄 **Match 46: RCB vs KKR – A Tale of Two PowerPlays**

The RCB juggernaut, riding high on a 5-match winning streak, ran into a KKR buzzsaw at a pace-friendly RCB venue. Winning the toss and electing to bowl first, RCB’s decision seemed validated early as they struck twice in the 4th over, reducing KKR to 33/2. But a composed 48 from WL Madsen and a cameo from RG Sharma (29) helped KKR limp to 143/9 — a total that looked 20 runs short. J Miyaji’s bizarre spell of 0/10 from 4 overs (17 dots!) kept RCB in the hunt.

Then came the chase. CH Gayle (39 off 14) exploded like only he can, racing RCB to 44/0 after 3 overs. But the arrival of B Kumar turned the game on its head. In a stunning 4th over, Bhuvi removed Gayle, Salil Arora, and Patidar for a golden duck each — a triple-wicket maiden of sorts. From 44/0, RCB slumped to 55/3, then 59/4. The middle order offered no resistance as Suryansh Shedge (3/17) cleaned up the tail. RCB were bowled out for 122 in just 13 overs — losing by 21 runs. A stunning collapse. 🔥

A heist. A choke. A classic.

**Winner:** KKR  
**Player of the Match:** Suryansh Shedge (KKR)
