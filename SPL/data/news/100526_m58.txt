# 🏏 SPL S07, Match 58 – PBKS vs CSK: The Suryavanshi Special & A Chase for the Ages

CSK won the toss and chose to bat first on a pace-friendly pitch. Gaikwad smashed two sixes in the first three balls... then holed out. That set the tone for a weird innings.

Sudharsan (24) and Tilak Varma (47) rebuilt steadily, but the real story was KK Nair. He stood like a rock amid falling partners, remaining unbeaten on **89 off 44 balls** with 6 sixes, dragging CSK to **201/6** — a solid but not insane total. Tanush Kotian's 13* off 3 balls gave them a late boost.

PBKS needed 202 to win. What followed was pure cinema.

V Suryavanshi walked out and never looked back. Seifert fell early (13), Rana crawled to 20 off 19, and at 98/2 after 12 overs, PBKS needed 104 off 48 — a near-impossible ask. Then the switch flipped.

Suryavanshi, who had been anchoring patiently, erupted. He tore into Chahal in the 19th over — **6,6,6,4,6,4** — a jaw-dropping **32 runs in a single over**. The equation vanished. With Mendis (31* off 15) providing furious support, PBKS chased down **202/3** in exactly 20 overs.

Suryavanshi finished **109* off 55 balls** — a century that will be remembered for years. PBKS won by 7 wickets with the last ball of the match.

**Winner:** PBKS by 7 wickets  
**Player of the Match:** V Suryavanshi (PBKS) – for a match-winning century
