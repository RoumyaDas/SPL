# 🏏 SPL S07, Match 53: PBKS vs RCB – A Bowling Masterclass Defends a Rocky Total

The pitch at PBKS was a fast bowler's paradise, offering 1.9x more assistance to pace than spin. RCB won the toss and elected to bat first, but what followed was a chaotic innings held together by two veteran masters.

RCB's innings was a rollercoaster. **Chris Gayle (83 off 48)** and **Rajat Patidar (80* off 47)** were the anchors amidst a collapse. Gayle smashed 11 fours and 2 sixes, while Patidar played a mature hand, but wickets tumbled around them. **JJ Smit (4/13)** was the wrecker-in-chief, bowling an incredible 3.25 economy spell that ripped through RCB's middle order, leaving them at a shaky 205/6.

Chasing 206, PBKS got off to a flying start. **Tim Seifert (89 off 49)** played the innings of his life, finding boundaries at will. He shared a 107-run opening stand with **Vaibhav Suryavanshi (46)** , and the chase looked comfortable. But **HJ Manenti (4/41)** had other plans. He triggered a dramatic collapse, taking key wickets including Seifert and Shashank Singh. PBKS fell into a spin trap, losing 6 wickets for just 87 runs in the middle overs, falling short by 11 runs in a heartbreaking finish.

**Result:** RCB wins by 11 runs  
**Player of the Match:** JJ Smit (RCB) – for his extraordinary 4/13 spell
