# 🏏 SPL S07 Match 66: RR vs CSK – A Nail-Biting Cliffhanger

CSK won the toss and elected to bowl first, hoping to exploit early movement. But Aniket Verma had other plans. He came out with an intent that screamed dominance, stitching a rapid 47-run stand with a struggling Stokes. Verma was a one-man wrecking ball, piercing gaps at will. Stokes played a decent hand of 23 before falling, bringing the big-hitting Dube to the crease.

Verma marched to 99 in breathtaking fashion – a masterclass of 14 fours and 3 sixes – only to be run out one short of a deserving century. That triggered a mini-collapse. Dube held one end like a rock, but wickets kept tumbling around him. From a commanding position, RR stumbled to 230/7, thanks to a late cameo from Loftie-Eaton (14 off 5) and Dube’s unbeaten 87 off 45.

Chasing 231, CSK’s reply was electric. Tilak Varma came out with fire in his eyes, stitching a 100+ stand with Faf du Plessis. Du Plessis played the perfect second fiddle, scoring 55 before falling. But Varma? He stood like a titan. Even as wickets fell in a heap – Klaasen golden duck, Gaikwad counterattacked but perished – Varma remained unmoved.

The equation boiled down to 17 off the last over. Ngidi held his nerve. Gaikwad was caught, Kotian smashed a boundary, but Tilak Varma stayed unbeaten on a stunning 111 off 54. CSK fell just 5 runs short in a heart-stopper. RR won by 5 runs.

**Winner:** RR  
**Player of the Match:** Tilak Varma (CSK) – for a monumental 111* in a losing cause.
