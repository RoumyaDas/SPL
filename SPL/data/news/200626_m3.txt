# 🏏 SPL S08 Match 3: LSG vs GT – A Chase for the Ages

The season's third match served up a run-fest that went down to the wire, showcasing the very best of T20 cricket's drama.

LSG won the toss and chose to bowl first, a decision that looked shaky when GT's openers came out firing. SV Samson was in imperious touch, smashing 10 fours in his 22-ball 48 before falling just short of a well-deserved half-century. Naman Dhir added a brisk 27, but it was the arrival of V Nigam that truly changed the complexion of the innings. Walking in at 116/4, he played a stunning hand, remaining unbeaten on 62 off just 30 deliveries, anchoring the innings and ensuring GT posted a daunting 209/6. H Ssenyondo was the pick of the bowlers with figures of 3/37, but the damage had been done.

The chase began with purpose. Shubman Gill, in sublime form, played a captain's knock, scoring a fluent 68 off 32 balls, while FH Allen provided early support with 19. When Allen fell at 44, LR Chethan kept the momentum going, but GT's bowlers, led by the exceptional B Muzarabani, kept striking back. Muzarabani was devastating, picking up 4 wickets, including the key scalps of Chethan, Porel, and Jurel in quick succession.

The pressure was mounting. At 116/5, with 93 runs still needed from 52 balls, the equation seemed to be tilting GT's way. But then came the partnership that would define the match. Sameer Rizvi, who had walked in at a precarious 100/4, found an able ally in HH Pandya. The duo combined for a match-winning stand of 54 runs, with Rizvi smashing 37 off 20 and Pandya playing the anchor role to perfection.

When Rizvi fell in the 17th over with 41 runs still needed, the game was in the balance. But Pandya, who had been biding his time, exploded in the 18th over, smashing Ashutosh Sharma for three consecutive sixes. He finished with an unbeaten 41 off 23 balls, guiding LSG to a thrilling victory with an over to spare. Harshit Rana's late cameo and the final over heroics sealed the deal, as LSG chased down 210 with 8 wickets in hand.

It was a match that had everything: centuries, hat-trick balls, incredible catches, and a finish that kept everyone on the edge of their seats. LSG's chase was a masterclass in calculated aggression, and they deservedly walked away with the win.
